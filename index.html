What is a Police Clearance?

A police clearance is an official document issued by the Philippine National Police (PNP) certifying that an individual has no criminal records within the National Police Clearance System. It is often required for:

  • Employment or job applications
  • Immigration or visa applications
  • Business permits or local transactions
  • Personal records or legal documentation

Unlike the NBI clearance, which covers nationwide records, a police clearance may be local (LPC) or national (NPC) depending on your purpose.

Types of Police Clearance in the Philippines

  • National Police Clearance (NPC): Covers criminal records nationwide. Required for employment, immigration, or official transactions. Validity: 3 months to 1 year.
  • Local Police Clearance (LPC): Covers only records within a specific locality. Usually faster and cheaper than NPC. Validity: 6 months.
  • Barangay Clearance (BC): Issued by local barangays confirming no pending cases in the community. Often used for local permits or minor transactions.

Why You Need a Police Clearance in 2026

  • Employment: Employers require it to verify a clean record.
  • Education: Schools or universities may need it for enrollment or programs.
  • Local Travel: Some LGUs require it for travel permits.
  • Business Transactions: Needed for licenses or permits.
  • Personal Documentation: Useful for future legal or official purposes.

Using a police clearance ensures compliance with legal requirements and smooth processing of applications.

Step-by-Step Guide to Police Clearance Online Registration 2026

Step 1: Create an NPCS Online Account

  • Visit the National Police Clearance System (NPCS) website.
  • Click Register and fill out personal information including full name, birthdate, address, and contact details.
  • Verify your account via email by clicking the activation link.

Step 2: Log in and Complete Your Profile

  • Log in to the NPCS website with your email and password.
  • Complete your profile with any additional required information.
  • Ensure all details are accurate to avoid delays.

Step 3: Schedule a Police Clearance Online Appointment

  • Click the Appointment tab in your account.
  • Select the nearest police station for processing.
  • Choose a date and time convenient for you.
  • Confirm your appointment details.

Step 4: Pay the Police Clearance Fee

The standard fee is ₱150. Payment methods include:

  • GCash: Mobile payment option.
  • Landbank: Online banking payment.
  • Over-the-Counter: Authorized payment centers.

Step 5: Visit the Police Station

  • Bring two valid IDs (original and photocopy).
  • Bring the payment receipt and printed application form (if required).
  • Submit documents, undergo biometrics, and wait for processing (usually 15–30 minutes).
  • Receive your Police Clearance certificate.

Requirements for Police Clearance in 2026

  • Two valid IDs with photo and signature (Passport, PhilID, Driver’s License, UMID)
  • Completed application form (for walk-ins)
  • Payment receipt of ₱150
  • 2x2 ID photos for biometric capture

Walk-In Police Clearance Application

  • Visit your local police station and request the application form.
  • Submit two valid IDs and pay the processing fee.
  • Undergo biometrics and photo capture.
  • Wait for the police clearance certificate.

Note: Walk-in processing may take longer due to queues.

Renewal of Police Clearance Online

  • Log in to your NPCS account.
  • Schedule a new appointment.
  • Pay the renewal fee.
  • Bring valid IDs and payment proof.
  • Update biometrics if required.
  • Receive your renewed police clearance certificate.

Processing Time and Delivery

  • Without HIT: Clearance is issued immediately after biometric verification.
  • With HIT: Additional verification may be required, extending processing time.
